# PILLAR 01 — HOME ORGANIZATION & MOVE SUPPORT
## Get the project moving.

Your home, made to work for how you actually live.

Usually, it starts with one problem. But most of the time, the hard part isn't actually the boxes. It's dealing with everything tied to them while life keeps moving around you.

The room that keeps collecting piles. The move that technically happened but never settled. The closet that no longer fits your life, body, season, or the way you actually get dressed (morning or afternoon — no judgment). The storage unit collecting procrastination tax.

Anyone can make a room look organized for a week. The difference here is who's standing in it. I've spent a career being handed inventory worth millions and deciding what stays, what goes, what sells, what's broken, and what's worth fixing — and that's the same eye that reads your hallway boxes, your closet, your garage. I'm not arranging your things. I'm assessing them, and building the room around what actually deserves the space.

### Moves: we take it off your hands
We handle the setup while you're at work. We do the Target run with the Landing Bundles. We steam the suit for tomorrow. We get you stocked with a week of whatever — so the first night in the new place has forks, sheets, chargers, and zero panic.

And when you have to leave before the move is finished — the new city, the family home, the furnished rental — we stay behind and close it out: pack what's left, ship what you need in labeled boxes, route what sells, return the keys.

### Ways we can work together
Side-by-side guided sessions · hybrid decision blocks · full key handoff · solo resets while you're away · packing and move prep with or without you · closet and system continuity after landing. You don't have to be present for your home to get better.

### The pile at the end
Every reset ends with a pile, and most organizers leave it with you — which is how the project quietly becomes unfinished again. Here, the pile gets read on the spot. What sells routes straight into Resale & Consignment. What donates leaves with me. And sometimes the thing you almost tossed is the piece a client of mine has been hunting for years.

# PILLAR 02 — HOUSE CALLS
## Help for the things that don't fit neatly into a category.

Practical help for the parts of life that quietly pile up.

Sometimes the issue is a closet that no longer functions. Sometimes it's an outdated smoke detector, an unreachable shelf, a laptop that stopped syncing years ago, or a room that's slowly become harder to move through comfortably. And sometimes, it's simply the reality that life has accumulated faster than anyone has had time to properly address.

House Calls are designed for exactly that space. Not crisis. Not caregiving. Not "having it all figured out." Just capable help for the operational side of everyday life — from the person you used to have to call.

### What a House Call can include
Reorganizing spaces for easier access · technology setup and troubleshooting · home safety updates and small operational fixes · organizing collections · clearer storage systems · resale and donation prep · simplified charging stations and lighting · post-move settling · support after surgery, loss, divorce, or major transition · documenting belongings for family clarity.

Half this job is doing the task. The other half is knowing, the moment I'm in the room, which task it actually is.

### One visit instead of four vendors
Vendors fix what you point at. The value of a House Call is what gets seen without being pointed at — the cord that's a fall risk, the detector that expired two years ago, the lighting that explains why she stopped using that room, the "TV problem" that's actually three remotes and one wrong input.

### For adult children managing from a distance
Many clients first reach out trying to help a parent from another city. Their parent is still independent. Still sharp. Still fully themselves. But the household started carrying more operational weight than one person should manage alone. A House Call creates trusted eyes on the ground — someone to notice what's working, what quietly stopped, what could get harder later, and what can be made easier now — without disrupting dignity or independence. One visit: phone updated, bulbs and switches made safe, detector dates checked, vents cleaned, TV down to one clicker, and a recorded walkthrough video for the next late-night emergency before I say *you've got this.*

### The goal is never perfection
The goal is relief. Competence does not eliminate the need to be cared for. And thoughtful support should not require a crisis first.

### Pricing
$175/hr · 2-hour minimum. **The 2-Hour House Call ($350) is the fast book.** Ongoing monthly hours available for continuity. No membership. No subscription. Ease comes from relationship, not a forced plan.

# PILLAR 03 — LEGACY INVENTORY & CATALOGING
## Understand what's there before decisions become urgent.

Most families have a plan for the money. Far fewer have a plan for everything else.

The collections. The furniture. The tools in the garage. The china nobody brings up anymore. The things with value, the things with history, the things everyone assumes someone else knows about — until a decision has to be made, and suddenly nobody's sure what's there, what matters, or where to begin.

### A home doesn't hold one kind of value. It holds five.
Financial. Historical. Practical. Personal. Family. And they get confused with each other constantly. The bag that looks expensive but isn't. The plain jacket quietly worth real money. The coat with market value *and* provenance *and* a story still attached. Most of what goes wrong in family transitions happens when one kind of value gets mistaken for another — something donated because nobody knew the story, something kept forever because somebody *only* knew the story.

This work tells those values apart — while the people who know the stories are still here to tell them.

### What a Legacy Inventory is
A documented record of a home: contents, collections, notable pieces, and the household knowledge that disappears exactly when it's needed. Room-by-room cataloging, photo documentation, valuation and resale routing, family distribution references, donation pathways, transition roadmap, printed and digital family copies.

### The Story Archive
When a piece has a story worth keeping with it, that history is documented too — and when it sells, the story travels with it. The next person who wears the coat or sets the table gets a second life, not just an object.

### The real reason to do it now: choice.
You stay the one deciding — what stays, what sells, what gets gifted to the person who'll actually love it, what gets remembered. The alternative is someone else making those calls later, fast, under pressure, with half the information. Not because they don't care — people can only hold what they recognize.

### Practical, not sentimental
Not a museum. Not keeping everything. Plenty of things should leave — and this work makes leaving easier, because every decision gets made with real information instead of guesswork.

### Pricing
$175/hr · 2-hour minimum (single room, storage unit, focused catalog). Whole home: project quote after walkthrough. Items identified for sale route directly into Resale & Consignment without restarting the process.

# PILLAR 04 — RESALE & CONSIGNMENT
## Turn the pile into action.

**You fill the bag. I figure out the rest.**

You make one decision: *I don't want this anymore.* I make the next fifty. Is it worth selling? Where — Poshmark, eBay, Etsy, Chairish, Vinted, Vestiaire, Grailed, Facebook Marketplace, a local dealer, a collector who's been hunting it for years? For how much? Worth shipping? Worth photographing? Vintage, designer, or just done? Most people get stuck before question three. That's why the pile is still in the closet.

### Fast Bag Fill — the easiest place to start
Fill a bag, box, or pickup pile with the clothing, accessories, shoes, decor, or collectibles you no longer want to manage. Pickup is complimentary within our service area — city pickup, route stop, courier, building desk drop, or UPS Access Point. We handle the next step: resale evaluation, platform placement, donation routing, and thoughtful dispersal. No photographing. No researching. No figuring out where things belong.

### More than clothing
Designer accessories · vintage and contemporary clothing · jewelry · art and framed pieces · lamps and decor · housewares · partial china sets · collections · inherited belongings · storage unit contents · transitional household overflow. Sometimes the value is financial. Sometimes it's making sure something useful, beautiful, or meaningful isn't unnecessarily discarded. Both matter.

### How it works (and the honest timeline)
Within 48 hours of pickup you'll get an inventory confirmation; within 7–10 business days you'll know what's accepted, what's borderline, and what's better donated. If something isn't worth listing, I'll tell you. Most strong items sell within the first 90 days; nearly everything with real demand finds its buyer within 6–9 months, with a check-in at 180 days. Resale rewards patience, not urgency — if immediate cash is the priority, resale isn't the right tool, and I'll say so up front.

### The part you're actually paying for
Not the listing. The judgment. What deserves effort, what gains value with its story attached, what belongs in the collector world, what should wait for its season, what's better donated. The goal is the right next life and the most realistic return — not the fastest sale.

Clothing & Accessories — you 45% / WLC 55% · Designer & Luxury — 50/50 · Furniture & Significant Home Pieces — 50/50 · Full Closet Liquidation — 45/55 · Low-value volume bags (items mostly in the $5–$10 range) — 35/65, reviewed and communicated at intake, never retroactive.

Commission is calculated on net proceeds after platform fees and approved costs. No upfront fees — commission is earned only when something sells. **Payouts land the first Monday after each 30-day period**, with a sales summary every cycle. Agreement signed at intake.

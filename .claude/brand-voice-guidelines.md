## 3. Voice Constants vs. Tone Flexes

**VOICE (never changes):**
1. The three-step emotional architecture: **notice reality → remove shame → introduce relief.** Every page, post, and policy follows it.
2. **Observational specificity** beats elevated language. The storage unit nobody opens > "meaningful transformation."
3. **The Kitchen Rule:** rewrite until it sounds like a trusted, intelligent person solving a real problem in an actual home.
4. **Recognition over performance.** The reader should think "Yes. Exactly." — not "That's beautifully written."
5. **The judgment thread.** Name the eye, not just the task — in every service, not only where it's obvious.
6. **Never** frame the business exclusively around grief, hardship, or crisis. Busy is as valid as grieving.
7. **No grief marketing** in family-transition contexts; acknowledge the practical aftermath, never claim legal estate work or therapy.

**TONE (flexes by context):** formality · energy · humor density · emotional proximity.

## 5. Brand Personality

The trusted person who's been handed million-dollar inventories, broken teams, fake Guccis, real St. Johns, and a Tuesday afternoon with Bob and his fanny pack — and made the right call on all of them. Sits in the middle most people can't occupy: understands resale AND objects AND people. Knows a daughter might throw something away because she doesn't know the story, and a parent might keep something because they only know the story — and that sometimes the right answer is neither keeping nor selling. It's documenting, transferring, gifting, finding the next person.

**Confidence: HIGH** — consistent across founder stream and both 6/12 sessions.

## 6. Language Rules

**Scrutiny-trigger list ("Brand Monster" words — not banned, but justify or replace):** stewardship · ecosystem · elevated · holistic · intentional · transformative · curated* · care-driven · seamless · aligned · luxury experience · white-glove · meaningful belongings · holding space · optimize · supportive container · lifestyle transformation.
*\*"Curated" survives only inside the locked service name "Curated Resale & Consignment."*

**MOVE-CONTENT VOICE RULE (Dayna, 6/12): "We take it off your hands."** Anything touching moves is phrased as us doing it while life keeps going: we handle the setup while you're at work · we do the Target run with the Landing Bundles · we steam the suit for tomorrow · we get you stocked with a week of whatever. The vibe is "we handle the things" — active, concrete, off-your-plate. Not "support for your move."

**Signature moves:**
- Lists of real objects, not categories: "the weird lamp, the storage unit, the resale pile."
- The shame-removal beat lands before the offer, always.
- One-line outcomes over feature lists: "The bags leave my house and I never have to think about them again."
- Honest constraint as trust-builder: timelines, what doesn't fit, what she'll say no to.

**The final test:** trusted person helping with a real problem → aligned. Keynote, luxury retreat, or emotionally branded LinkedIn post → rewrite.

**Confidence: HIGH** — verbatim from locked BRAND-VOICE.md.

## 7. Content Examples

**ON-VOICE:**
- "Most clients hire me because the operational side of life quietly became heavier than it used to be."
- "You make one decision: *I don't want this anymore.* I make the next fifty."
- "The move that technically happened six months ago."
- "No membership. No subscription. Ease comes from relationship, not a forced plan."

**OFF-VOICE (and why):**
- "Objects carry emotional continuity." → abstraction replacing observation.
- "A white-glove concierge experience for the modern household." → aggressively luxurious, category-wrong.
- "We hold space for your transition journey." → therapy-coded.
- "Dayna matched your Gucci bag to the appropriate resale platform." → describes the task, not the outcome; nobody talks like this.
